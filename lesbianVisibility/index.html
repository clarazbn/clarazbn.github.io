Is there an ongoing lesbian renaissance?

From Chappell Roan to Rennee Rapp, and Kristen Stewart - lesbians are becoming more visible in mainstream media, and have been for the past two decades. Does that mean that we are finally out of the woods?

by Clara Zimban

In the last 25 years, more than 450 movies and TV shows have featured sapphic characters and relationships. This is roughly nine times more representation than available in all previous decades combined.

Although lesbians are placed at the forefront of the LGBTQ+ acronym, they have long been marginalized and invisibilized within and outside of the community as a result of their complex intersectional identity.

The 2010s, specifically, have been an era of queer awakening for mainstream broadcast channels. Critics have rewarded Jane the Virgin(2014), Carol (2015), Orange is the New Black(2013), and many other queer pieces as some of the best movies and tv shows of the decade.

Until recently, gay men still made up the large majority of regular and recurring LGBTQ+ characters on television.

In 2021, for the first time, lesbians outnumbered gay men on broadcast TV.
